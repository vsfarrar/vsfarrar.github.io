Victoria Farrar, Ph.D.

I am currently a National Science Foundation Postdoctoral Research Fellow in Biology, working with Dr. Alison Bell at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign. I am hatching plans to study the neural and physiological changes associated with the recent loss of parental care in a population of three-spined stickleback (Gasterosteus aculeatus).

I recieved my Ph.D. in Animal Behavior at the University of California Davis, where I worked with Dr. Rebecca Calisi Rodríguez. I investigated the neuroendocrine basis of reproduction and stress responses during parental care. Learn more about my dissertation research here.

I also engaged in discipline-based education research (DBER), and am passionate about science education and communication. You can learn more about my DBER work here.
